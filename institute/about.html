About the Institute

What this is, and what it isn't.

The Gloxx QA Institute is the published methodology behind the Gloxx Retainer — workflows, a maturity rubric, a free readiness self-assessment, and a paid readiness audit. It is a publication and a service, not a regulatory body. Everything here is described in concrete terms so you can run it yourself, or hire us to run it on your code.

What the Institute publishes

Six AI-QA workflows, each documented as a procedure with named artifacts and observable signals. The AI-QA Maturity Model as the rubric the workflows are scored against. The readiness self-assessment as the free-tier diagnostic. Open-source tools that scaffold the workflows in client codebases. A journal of opinionated long-form, and quarterly reports with anonymized aggregate data.

  • FreeReadiness Self-Assessment. Five-minute online instrument. Returns your AI-QA maturity level and your weakest workflow. Same rubric the audit anchors to.

Audit, not certification — see non-warranty section below.

What the Institute is not

Gloxx is not a regulatory body. The Institute does not certify compliance, does not issue credentials to individuals, and is not a member of any standards organization. The vocabulary on this site — "audit," "review," "rubric," "level" — is methodological, not regulatory.

The published methodology and the audit deliverables describe what we observed and how we scored it against our rubric, at a point in time, on the artifacts provided. They are not warranties, attestations, or guarantees of any outcome.

Standard non-warranty

Audit, not certification. The AI-QA Readiness Audit is a methodology-based assessment of release readiness at a point in time. It is not a regulatory certification, compliance attestation, or warranty of any outcome.

The Gloxx QA Institute is a publisher of methodology and a service provider; it is not a regulatory or standards body.

Audit reports are dated, scoped to the artifacts reviewed, and explicit about what was not reviewed. Findings reflect the reviewer's professional judgment against the published rubric — not a guarantee of outcomes.
